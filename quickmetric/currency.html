Conversion Factors

    Conversion factor: The rate to exchange one currency for another.

    Market-driven: Changes frequently based on market conditions, economic events, and supply-demand.

    Direct conversion: Example: If 1 USD = 0.85 EUR, USD to EUR conversion factor is 0.85.

    Inverse conversion: To convert back, use the inverse rate. In the example, EUR to USD = 1 ÷ 0.85 = 1.18.

    Application: Multiply the amount by the conversion factor to convert between currencies.

Definition of Currency

Currency is a standardized system of money used as a medium of exchange for goods and services in an economy. It is issued and regulated by a government or central authority and holds a specific value recognized within and outside a country. Currencies come in various forms, such as coins, banknotes, or digital currency. Common examples include the US Dollar (USD), Euro (EUR), and Japanese Yen (JPY).

Common formulas for Currency Conversion:

  • Direct Conversion: Converted Amount = Amount × Conversion Rate
  • Inverse Conversion: Conversion Rate (Inverse) = 1 ÷ Direct Conversion Rate
  • Cross Rate Conversion (for two currencies through a third currency): Cross Rate = (Currency A to Currency C) ÷ (Currency B to Currency C)
  • Percentage Change in Exchange Rate: Percentage Change = ((New Rate - Old Rate) ÷ Old Rate) × 100

Understanding currency is essential for global trade, financial markets, and economic stability. It enables international transactions and exchange rate management between economies.

Foreign Currencies

Dollar (USD):

The official currency of the United States, widely used as a global reserve currency. It is subdivided into 100 cents and is symbolized by the dollar sign ($).

Euro (EUR):

The common currency used by 19 of the 27 European Union member countries, collectively known as the Eurozone. It is the second most traded currency in the world and is also subdivided into 100 cents.

Pound Sterling (GBP):

The official currency of the United Kingdom and its territories, recognized by the symbol "£". It is one of the oldest currencies still in use and is divided into 100 pence.

Yen (JPY):

The official currency of Japan, represented by the symbol "¥". The yen is one of the most traded currencies globally and is not subdivided into smaller units.

Rupee (INR):

The official currency of India, denoted by the symbol "₹". It is subdivided into 100 paise and is used as a medium of exchange for goods and services in the Indian economy.

Won (KRW):

The official currency of South Korea, represented by the symbol "₩". The won is subdivided into 100 jeon and is used in domestic and international trade.

Franc (CHF):

The official currency of Switzerland and Liechtenstein, recognized by the symbol "Fr" or "SFr". The franc is subdivided into 100 rappen (or centimes in French) and is known for its stability.

Peso (MXN):

The official currency of Mexico, symbolized by "$" or "MX$" to distinguish it from other dollar-denominated currencies. It is subdivided into 100 centavos and is used in various economic transactions within the country.

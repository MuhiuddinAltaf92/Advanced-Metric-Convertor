Definition of Mass

Mass is the measure of the amount of matter in an object, typically expressed in units such as grams (g), kilograms (kg), or pounds (lb). It quantifies how much substance or material an object contains, regardless of its volume or the gravitational force acting on it.

Common formulas for calculating mass include:

  • Density Formula: Mass = Density × Volume
  • Force Formula: Mass = Force ÷ Acceleration (Newton's Second Law)
  • Weight Formula: Mass = Weight ÷ Gravitational Acceleration

Units of Mass

Gram

A gram is the metric unit of mass, commonly used to measure small quantities of material.

Kilogram

The kilogram is the base unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI) and equals 1,000 grams.

Pound

A pound is a unit of mass used in the imperial system. It is equivalent to 0.453592 kilograms.

Ounce

An ounce is a smaller unit of mass in the imperial system, with 16 ounces equaling one pound.

Metric Ton

The metric ton is a unit of mass equal to 1,000 kilograms or approximately 2,204.62 pounds.

Milligram

A milligram is a metric unit of mass, equivalent to 0.001 grams, commonly used for small masses.

Microgram

A microgram is used to measure extremely small masses. One microgram equals 0.000001 grams.

Stone

The stone is a unit of mass used in the U.K. One stone is equivalent to 14 pounds or 6.35 kilograms.

Faqs of Mass Conversion

To convert weight:
1 Ounce = 28.3495 Grams
Formula:
Grams = Ounces × 28.3495
Ounces = Grams ÷ 28.3495

To convert weight:
1 Pound = 0.453592 Kilograms
Formula:
Kilograms = Pounds × 0.453592
Pounds = Kilograms ÷ 0.453592

To convert mass:
1 Short Ton = 0.907185 Megagrams (Metric Tons)
Formula:
Metric Tons = Short Tons × 0.907185
Short Tons = Metric Tons ÷ 0.907185

Pound: Commonly used in the US, 1 pound = 16 ounces = 0.453592 kilograms.
Short Ton: Used in the US, equals 2,000 pounds (907.185 kilograms).
These units are part of the Imperial and US customary systems.

Ounce to Gram: Multiply by 28.3495.
